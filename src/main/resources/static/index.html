Heritage of India

A timeline of monuments that tell the story of India’s rich past.

Taj Mahal, Agra

Built: 1632 – 1653

A monument of love in white marble, commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan for Mumtaz Mahal.

Qutub Minar, Delhi

Construction: late 12th – early 13th century

One of the tallest brick minarets in the world, marking the beginning of Delhi Sultanate rule.

Hampi, Karnataka

Flourished: 14th – 16th century

Capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, filled with temples, bazaars, and royal complexes in stone.

Jaipur City Palace, Rajasthan

Built: early 18th century

A blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture, showcasing royal courts, museums, and courtyards.

Wildlife of India

From Bengal tigers to one-horned rhinos, explore India’s diverse national parks.

Jim Corbett National Park

Uttarakhand · Tigers · Safaris

India’s oldest national park, famous for Bengal tiger sightings and dense sal forests.

Ranthambore National Park

Rajasthan · Tigers · Fort Ruins

Dramatic landscapes with an old fort in the backdrop – perfect for wildlife & photography.

Kaziranga National Park

Assam · One-horned Rhinos

A UNESCO site known for its population of Indian one-horned rhinoceros and rich birdlife.
